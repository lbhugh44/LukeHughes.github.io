Research Statement

My research interests center on understanding the causal relationship between regular aerobic exercise and cardiovascular disease risk reduction in middle-aged adults. This line of inquiry is motivated by growing epidemiological evidence suggesting that even modest levels of physical activity exert significant protective effects against chronic disease.

I am particularly interested in identifying the mechanistic pathways through which exercise exerts its effects on cardiovascular health, as well as the degree to which this relationship is moderated by variables such as diet, socioeconomic status, and access to preventive care. Establishing a clearer causal link between these two discrete phenomena would have meaningful implications for the design of preventive health interventions targeting underserved populations.

My long-term research agenda is oriented toward translational medicine — ensuring that findings generated at the bench level are systematically applied to improve clinical outcomes and public health policy. I view rigorous experimental design and transparent statistical methodology as essential prerequisites for generating evidence that is both internally valid and practically actionable.
